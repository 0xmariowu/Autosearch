## Content types

Academic papers with structured metadata such as title, abstract, authors, year, venue hints, and citation counts. It is a paper-discovery channel, not a full-text research archive.

## Language

Best in English. Some Chinese papers appear, but English coverage is much stronger and query matching is materially better for English research terms.

## Best for

Literature surveys, finding foundational papers, getting a fast view of a research area, and prioritizing papers by citation impact. It is especially good when you want paper metadata with less noise than general web search.

## Blind spots

It does not provide code, tutorials, blog posts, product news, or practitioner writeups. Coverage can be incomplete for niche fields, and some papers have thin metadata or missing abstracts.

## Quality signals

Citation count is the key signal for impact. Strong abstract match, recognizable authors, and publication year aligned with the query help separate core papers from marginal matches.
