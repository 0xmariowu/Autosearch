## Content types

General web pages: docs, blog posts, company pages, news articles, landing pages, forums, and whatever else DuckDuckGo has indexed. This is the broadest channel and the least opinionated about source type.

## Language

Best in English, with some support for other languages depending on DuckDuckGo indexing. Multilingual queries can work, but quality is inconsistent compared with dedicated Chinese or academic channels.

## Best for

General fallback search, especially when no specialized channel clearly fits or when you want to catch content those channels miss. It is useful for broad discovery, official docs, and mixed-source reconnaissance.

## Blind spots

Precision is low. It has no built-in notion of source quality, no structured metadata, and no guarantee that the best result ranks first. It is also weaker for walled gardens, fresh social content, and deeply niche technical discussions.

## Quality signals

Quality varies wildly, so domain trust matters more than rank position alone. Official sites, strong title overlap, and snippets containing the exact constraint or entity are better signals than vague keyword matches.
