## Content types

Professional profiles, company pages, job listings, and public posts or articles from LinkedIn. In practice, most results are shallow public previews rather than rich page content.

## Language

Mostly English. The professional metadata is often standardized enough to work across names and titles, but English search terms still perform best.

## Best for

Industry perspectives, job-market signals, basic executive or company-page discovery, and professional opinion scanning. It can help answer "who is talking about this in a business context" more than "what is the best technical source."

## Blind spots

Search quality is poor, a lot of content is self-promotional, and public indexing only exposes a thin slice of LinkedIn. It is not good for deep research, technical truth-finding, or comprehensive people search.

## Quality signals

Quality is generally weak here, so exact entity match matters most. Prefer direct profile or company pages over vague aggregated result pages, and treat engagement or authority claims cautiously because the indexed view is surface-level.
