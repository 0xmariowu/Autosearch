## Content types

Research preprints from arXiv with titles, abstracts, authors, categories, and submission timestamps. This is direct paper metadata for pre-publication work, not commentary about the paper.

## Language

Primarily English. Queries should generally use English research terminology to get reliable matches.

## Best for

Latest research, especially in ML, AI, and computer science, where arXiv often shows work before formal publication. It is the right channel when recency matters more than peer-review status.

## Blind spots

There is no peer-review signal here, no code quality signal, and no guarantee the work was ever published elsewhere. Coverage is also uneven outside arXiv-heavy fields, so it is not a universal academic search engine.

## Quality signals

Recency matters a lot on arXiv. Strong abstract overlap helps, and author reputation can matter in practice, but that reputation is not explicit in the returned metadata.
